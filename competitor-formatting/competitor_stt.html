How well do STT providers format entities?

A benchmark of six speech-to-text systems on built-in smart formatting.

Every speech-to-text provider ships some kind of "smart formatting" — the layer that turns "one seventy one forty one" into "$171.41". We tested how well it actually works.

We generated 160 audio clips with Deepgram Aura-2 TTS — 10 clips for each of 16 entity classes — and ran every clip through six STT services. Each transcript was graded by two independent judges (Claude Opus 4.7 and GPT-5.5) on whether the entities came back correctly formatted.

Overall results

"Pass" means both judges agreed the entity formatting was correct. Latency is single-call (one audio file at a time).

ProviderModel Pass p50 p90 p99
Sonioxstt-async-preview 50% 2,617 ms4,349 ms4,532 ms
OpenAIgpt-4o-transcribe 42% 576 ms961 ms3,026 ms
AzureFast Transcription 2024-11 39% 792 ms952 ms1,165 ms
Deepgramnova-3 + smart_format 34% 150 ms270 ms1,133 ms
ElevenLabsscribe_v1 32% 545 ms832 ms1,155 ms
GoogleCloud Speech v1 22% 723 ms1,338 ms1,697 ms

Two stories here. Soniox spends extra time and wins on accuracy — it's the only provider above 50%. Deepgram is the fastest by an order of magnitude (150 ms vs the next-fastest at 545 ms) but trades that for less aggressive formatting.

A few patterns jump out:

  • TIME and SSN are universally broken. No provider gets past 30% on either. Time-of-day formatting ("3:30 PM" vs "15:30") and SSN dash-grouping are formatting choices the STT layer apparently doesn't make confidently.
  • Credit cards are nearly unusable. Best score is 2/10 — the 16-digit grouping rarely survives transcription.
  • Soniox specifically targets medical and date intervals. 9/10 on DRUG_WITH_DOSE and 9/10 on DATE_INTERVAL, far above everyone else.
  • Deepgram dominates ADVERSARIAL. 5/10 vs 1–4 for everyone else — its smart formatter is more conservative, so it leaks fewer prompt-injection artifacts.
  • Google trails on anything that needs more than punctuation. Cloud Speech v1's automatic-punctuation layer is solid but it doesn't try entity normalization.

The dual judge

Every transcript was scored independently by Claude Opus 4.7 and GPT-5.5. The two judges agreed on 91–95% of rows, depending on the provider:

Strong agreement at the row level. The "both pass" number in the overall table is the conservative count — we only call a row good when both judges call it good.

Compared to a formatting LLM on top

For context, on the same kind of test set, our fine-tuned Qwen3.5-9B (running as a post-processing LLM after a raw Deepgram transcript) scored 92%.

The best provider's built-in smart formatting (Soniox at 50%) is still 42 points behind what a small fine-tuned LLM can do on the same audio. The formatting LLM also runs about 5× faster end-to-end than Soniox does at synthesizing the answer in one pass.

What we'd do

If you can only buy STT
Soniox or OpenAI

Soniox is the most accurate out of the box. OpenAI's gpt-4o-transcribe is the best speed/accuracy trade in the middle of the pack.

If you can run an LLM after
Deepgram + fine-tune

Raw Deepgram nova-3 transcripts at 150 ms, plus a fine-tuned 9B post-processor, gets to 92% — almost double the best pure-STT smart-formatter and still well under a second total.

Benchmark: 160 audio clips (10 per entity class across 16 classes) generated with Deepgram Aura-2 TTS, transcribed by six STT services, judged independently by Claude Opus 4.7 and GPT-5.5.
